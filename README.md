# Aquisight 360° — Company Purchasing Intelligence

## 📌 Project Overview

The aim of this project was to design and develop an interactive Power BI dashboard to visualize and analyze the company’s purchasing data. This enables key stakeholders to understand expenditure trends, optimize procurement strategies, evaluate supplier performance, and identify cost-saving opportunities. The dataset comprises 500 transaction records with fields such as item details, cost metrics, purchase dates, suppliers, and buyers.

---

## 📂 Data Source

The dataset was sourced from [Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/), a reputable platform for open datasets used in data science projects.

---

## 🛠️ Tools and Methodology

**Power BI**: Used for preliminary data cleaning, exploration, analysis, and dashboard creation

## 🔍 Observations & Insights

- **Total Spend: $1.24M**  
  The company’s cumulative procurement cost reached $1.24 million, indicating a high volume of purchasing activity across multiple suppliers and categories.

- **Total Quantity Purchased: 4,820 Units**  
  A significant number of items were procured, suggesting steady inventory turnover and consistent operational needs.

- **Top Buyer – Walter Pena ($96.42K)**  
  Walter Pena emerged as the top spender, followed closely by Jasmine McGee. Monitoring high-expenditure buyers can help assess purchasing behaviors and compliance with procurement policies.

- **Most Purchased Items – Laptop Bags (652), Desk Chairs (623), Staplers (622)**  
  These items dominated total quantities, signaling either frequent use or strategic stocking. Bulk purchase patterns may offer opportunities for supplier negotiation.

- **Aggregate Cost by Category – Electronics ($697.81K), Software ($336.02K), Furniture ($172.82K)**  
  Electronics constitute the majority of spend, accounting for over 56% of total purchases. Software and Furniture follow, revealing core investment areas.

- **Top Suppliers – TechMart Inc. ($328.76K), QuickDeliver Ltd. ($285.35K)**  
  The concentration of procurement among a few suppliers highlights potential supplier dependency. It also offers leverage for volume-based discounts.

- **Monthly Trend – Spend Peaks in April and September**  
  Noticeable spikes occur in April and September, suggesting periodic procurement cycles, possibly aligned with budgeting, project initiations, or operational ramp-ups.

---

## ✅ Recommendations

- **Implement Spend Control Mechanisms**  
  Establish quarterly budget reviews to evaluate spend categories and mitigate overspending, especially in Electronics and Software.

- **Monitor and Audit High-Spending Buyers**  
  Introduce policy training and audit trails for top buyers like Walter Pena and Jasmine McGee to ensure alignment with procurement strategies.

- **Negotiate Bulk Purchase Discounts**  
  Engage in long-term vendor agreements or tender processes for high-volume items like laptop bags and chairs to reduce unit costs.

- **Diversify Supplier Base**  
  Reduce supplier concentration risk by sourcing secondary suppliers for high-value categories currently dominated by a few vendors.

- **Align Procurement with Seasonal Trends**  
  Leverage historical purchasing trends to optimize stock levels and resource planning, especially before high-spending months like April and September.
